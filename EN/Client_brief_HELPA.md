# Research Brief

**Brand Name Testing for Digital Lending Service in India**

---

## 1. Background & Context

We are an international fintech company planning to launch **a digital lending service** in India.

**The brand planned for market entry is "Helpa".** Before market entry, we need to validate how this brand name is received by the **actual target borrowers** in India.

All visual materials required for the study, including:

- the brand logo,
- app UI prototypes,
- and Google Play store page mockups,

are provided via the following Google Drive link:
https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1Jf4Y8yjtGFIKKzG6vwBxaK5VzzdkNo5z

These materials should be used during the sessions as key stimuli for evaluating brand perception, trust, and willingness to install and use the app.

The research will inform:

- Go / no-go decision on brand usage in India
- Potential risks (trust, associations, misinterpretations)
- Brand refinement (tone, promise, positioning cues)

Our competitors: RupeeRedee, Anytime Rupee, NIRA, CASHe, PaySense, mPokket, PayRupik, Rupeeontime, RapidRupee.

---

## 2. Research Objectives

### Primary Objectives

1. Assess **brand acceptance and cultural fit** of the name *Helpa* in India as a lending service
2. Evaluate **trust perception**: does the brand feel legitimate, safe, and appropriate for borrowing money
3. Identify **positive and negative associations** triggered by the name
4. Understand whether *Helpa* signals:
   - Financial help
   - Emergency support
   - Informal / formal lending
   - Local / foreign brand

### Secondary Objectives

5. Test **memorability and pronunciation**
6. Explore **emotional tone** (reassuring vs suspicious, friendly vs aggressive, etc.)
7. Compare *Helpa* against:
   - Generic Indian digital lending brands
   - "Too aggressive" or "too corporate" competitors (perceived, not necessarily named)

## 4. Methodology Expectations

We are open to agency recommendations, but **preferred formats** are:

### Option A (Preferred)

- **Online or in-person focus groups**
- 5–6 participants per group
- 3–4 groups total
- Split by:
  - Income bands
  - City tier
  - Prior negative lending experience vs neutral

### Option B (Supplementary)

- 1-on-1 **in-depth interviews (IDIs)**
- Especially useful for:
  - Trust & fear drivers
  - Brand rejection signals
  - Sensitive experiences with lenders

### Mandatory Requirements

- Borrower experience must be **validated**, not self-claimed
- Moderator must have **experience in financial services / lending research**
- Sessions conducted in **local language(s)** where appropriate, with English summaries

## 5. Key Discussion Areas / Stimuli

During the research, respondents will be shown:

- The brand name **Helpa** (standalone)
- **2–3 app UI prototypes** (core screens only)
- **Google Play store listing mockups** (icon, screenshots, short description)

The goal is to evaluate **holistic trust and conversion perception**, not UI aesthetics.

### 5.1 Brand Name — Blind & Contextual

**Without any explanation:**

1. What kind of app do you think this is?
2. What do you think it helps with?
3. Is this a *serious* app or something risky?
4. Would you click on it in Google Play? Why / why not?

**After clarification that it is a lending app:**

5. Does the name fit a money-lending service?
6. Does it feel:
   - Trustworthy / suspicious
   - Friendly / aggressive
   - Local / foreign
7. Would you feel comfortable borrowing money from an app with this name?

### 5.2 App Prototypes — Trust & Usability Signals

**Key instruction to moderator:**
Focus on *signals*, not visual beauty.

Questions:

1. Does this app look like a real financial service?
2. What makes it feel trustworthy or untrustworthy?
3. At what moment would you start hesitating to continue?
4. Which elements increase confidence?
5. Do you see any "red flags" that remind you of bad lending apps?

### 5.3 Google Play Store Page — Install Decision

Respondents should imagine they are **searching for a loan app right now**.

Questions:

1. Would you install this app from Google Play? Why / why not?
2. What is the *first thing* you look at on this page?
3. Does the app icon look:
   - Professional
   - Cheap
   - Scam-like
4. Do screenshots increase or decrease trust?
5. What would make you close the page immediately?

### 5.4 Brand–Product Consistency

1. Does the app **look like something called "Helpa"**?
2. Is there any mismatch between:
   - Name
   - Interface
   - Store description
3. If the app had a different name, would it feel more or less trustworthy?
4. What kind of person do you think this app is *for*?

### 5.5 Conversion & Fear Mapping

1. At which step would you most likely drop off?
2. What fears appear while looking at:
   - The name
   - The app screens
   - The store page
3. What would you need to see to feel safe enough to apply?

### 5.6 Final Verdict

1. Would you personally use this app?
2. Would you recommend it to a friend?
3. One word to describe this brand
4. One thing you would **change immediately**

### Mandatory Moderator Instruction (Very Important)

The discussion **must actively probe** for past negative experiences with loan apps.

Superficial "looks fine" answers are **not acceptable**.

## 6. Expected Deliverables

Expected outputs:

1. **Structured insight report** (English) + video recordings of the focus group sessions
2. Clear recommendation:
   - Use as is / adapt / reject
3. Key risks & mitigation ideas
4. Verbatim quotes (translated)
5. Optional:
   - Brand perception map (trust vs friendliness vs legitimacy)
   - Name variants or phonetic adaptations, only if they are spontaneously suggested by users during the research sessions

## 8. Success Criteria

The research is successful if it:

- Reflects **real borrower psychology**, not aspirational users
- Clearly answers: *"Can Helpa safely and credibly lend money in India?"*
- Identifies **early-stage brand risks before launch**
